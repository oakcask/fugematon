# Generator Constraint Rebuild

## Status

In progress. The completed slices have added constraint evidence and removed several focused repair surfaces. The latest slice expands continuation section-CSP candidate domains before score adoption, so continuation selection can compare support ordering and structural anchor support variants. The architecture target is still not complete: the standard review bundle still has 19 of 22 seeds at a review or infeasible relaxation level, and score-level support cleanup still remains active fallback evidence.

The next target is to classify and repair the remaining density-floor and non-chord structural-support windows exposed by the candidate-domain slice. The generator should keep searching alternate section plans, voice-slot assignments, rest reasons, support lines, and local harmonic realizations before accepting a section. Final or score-level repair may remain only as diagnostics-visible fallback evidence, not as the normal path that makes an otherwise invalid section acceptable.

## Preservation Contract

The rewrite preserves the public `GenerationOutput` event envelope, diagnostics compatibility, seed determinism, known voice set, safe tick and MIDI value shape, subject and answer plan identity, key metadata consistency, and the active `WritingProfile` pitch/range continuation contract.

Legacy exact note sequences and beauty-metric expected values are not preservation requirements. They may change when the solver gives the same structural guarantees directly. Review-only metrics such as line agency, lockstep, phrase recurrence, unison duration, local sentinels, and surface brilliance remain adoption evidence unless a later source-backed plan promotes a focused rule.

## Architecture Target

Continuation generation must construct bounded local `SectionConstraintProblem` instances before notes are committed to the score. Each section candidate must include:

* voice/time slots classified as `note`, `hold`, or internal `intentional-rest`;
* allowed rest reasons tied to section function, cadence, entry handoff, register relief, suspension resolution, or pedal thinning;
* required active-voice coverage for non-cadential continuation windows;
* structural root / chord-tone support at harmonic anchors;
* entry handoff and subject-fragment continuity constraints;
* independent contour / rhythm soft costs;
* deterministic candidate ids, hard-failure lists, soft-cost reasons, relaxation level, and bounded search width.

Backtracking is the desired behavior when a candidate creates unexplained silence or unsupported texture collapse. The search should first try alternative voicing, support-line, rest-reason, register, and local-harmony assignments inside the section. If all bounded candidates fail, the selected section must expose the relaxation reason in `constraintSatisfactionReview` and `generatorSearchTrace`; it must not be silently repaired after generation.
