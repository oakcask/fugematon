# Literature Map

Use this as a routing guide for literature research. Verify specific editions, page numbers, quotes, and bibliographic details before citing them in durable docs.

## Counterpoint And Fugue

Start here when reviewing voice independence, parallels, consonance/dissonance, leap recovery, subject/answer treatment, and fugue form.

* Fux-style species counterpoint: consonance/dissonance hierarchy, preparation and resolution, contrary and oblique motion, treatment of perfect intervals, melodic leap recovery.
* Renaissance and strict counterpoint studies: when a generated line should be judged against a stricter vocal-polyphony model.
* Bach fugue analysis and tonal counterpoint: exposition order, real/tonal answer, counter-subject identity, invertible counterpoint, episode sequencing, stretto, cadence pacing.

## Classical Tonal Harmony

Use for functional harmony, cadences, tendency tones, voice leading, modulation, sequence, phrase model, and form.

* Common-practice harmony and voice-leading texts.
* Formenlehre and phrase-rhythm sources when reviewing phrase boundary, continuation, cadence timing, and formal repetition.
* Schenkerian or prolongational perspectives only when they help explain longer-range contour or bass support; do not turn them into automatic hard constraints without project approval.

## Jazz And Modal Harmony

Use when reviewing extensions, avoid notes, modal color, guide tones, chord-scale fit, reharmonization-like behavior, and style profiles that intentionally depart from strict classical treatment.

* Jazz harmony and chord-scale theory for sevenths, extensions, altered tones, guide-tone lines, avoid-note handling, and tension resolution.
* Modal jazz and modal theory for characteristic tones, static harmony, and coloristic treatment of dissonance.
* Treat jazz tolerance for color tones as style-dependent; it does not automatically justify unresolved clashes in strict-classical settings.

## Popular Music

Use when reviewing loop-based repetition, groove, hook, bass-line behavior, texture density, phrase periodicity, and listener fatigue over long generation.

* Popular-music harmony and form studies for loop syntax, four-chord cycles, modal mixture, sectional contrast, and phrase regularity.
* Groove and rhythm studies for syncopation, backbeat, rhythmic stratification, and repeated patterns that sound intentional rather than mechanical.
* Production and arrangement perspectives for texture density, register spacing, doubling, and long-duration variation.

## Evaluation Of Generative Music

Use when changing review methodology, preference data, learned weights, or listening gates.

* Generative-music evaluation surveys for the distinction between objective metrics, subjective listening review, system-use evaluation, and pairwise preference.
* Music information retrieval metrics only when they answer the musical question; do not substitute a proxy score for a theory or listening claim.

## Source Hygiene

* Prefer primary texts, authoritative editions, peer-reviewed work, university materials, and reputable music-theory publishers.
* Use secondary summaries only to locate concepts, not as the only basis for durable project policy.
* Record source family even when exact citations are not available, for example "Fux/species counterpoint", "common-practice harmony", "jazz chord-scale theory", or "popular-music loop/form studies".
* When sources disagree, describe the style boundary instead of flattening the disagreement into one rule.
